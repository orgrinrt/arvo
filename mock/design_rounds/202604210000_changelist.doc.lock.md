## Purpose

Tiny design clarification before arvo-bitmask impl. The existing
DESIGN.md.tmpl in `mock/crates/arvo-bitmask/` is almost ready for
mechanical implementation; one piece needs tightening: what
concretely is `W` in `Mask<W>`?

The DESIGN text says "W is a type satisfying the arvo-bits
`BitSequence + BitAccess` contracts". In the arvo-bits impl round
(just closed), `BitSequence` / `BitAccess` got concrete impls on
`UFixed<I, F, S>` and `IFixed<I, F, S>` — not on raw primitives.
So `W` in `Mask<W>` is concretely one of the arvo-bits semantic
aliases: `QWord<Hot>` = `UFixed<{IBits::SIXTYFOUR}, {FBits::ZERO}, Hot>`
for `Mask64`, and a `[QWord<Hot>; 4]` backing array for `Mask256`.

This round clarifies that in `mock/crates/arvo-bitmask/DESIGN.md.tmpl`
so the concrete-type expectations are spelled out before impl.

## Per-crate doc changes

### arvo-bitmask — `mock/crates/arvo-bitmask/DESIGN.md.tmpl`

Update the "Bitmask types" section to spell out:

- `Mask64` is `Mask<QWord<Hot>>` where `QWord<Hot>` is the arvo-bits alias for `UFixed<64-bit, 0-fractional, Hot>`. The one-word payload is the `u64` backing `QWord<Hot>`.
- `Mask256` is a `[QWord<Hot>; 4]` backing — four 64-bit words with unrolled set-ops.
- The `Mask<W>` surface stays generic so a consumer could substitute a different bit-bearing width (e.g. `DWord<Hot>` for a 32-bit bitmask). `Mask64` / `Mask256` are the two we ship.

No other per-crate DESIGN template changes this round.
