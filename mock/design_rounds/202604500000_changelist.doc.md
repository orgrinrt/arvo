> ## Bench infrastructure
>
> arvo carries a bench infrastructure under `mock/benches/` modeled
> on the mockspace self-bench. The bench bin orchestrates variant
> cdylibs through the `mockspace-bench-harness` orchestrator + worker
> + dlopen pipeline; results land in CSV plus a `findings.md` report.
>
> The first bench compares `arvo_hash::Fnv1a` and
> `arvo_hash::XxHash3` at input sizes 64, 256, 1024, and 4096 bytes,
> with `outputs_may_differ = true` since the algorithms are
> independent. Variants are nested workspace members under
> `mock/benches/variants/`. Run with `cargo run --release` from
> inside `mock/benches/`.
>
> Bench discipline lives in mockspace's bench docs; arvo's role
> here is to be a real consumer of the harness and to surface
> measurement evidence behind the strategy / hash decisions arvo
> makes.

# Why this doc CL is small

Bench infrastructure is operational tooling, not architecture. The
arvo design templates already establish the substrate's identity,
layer structure, and discipline. Bench harness adoption is "we use
the framework mockspace ships"; it earns a paragraph in DESIGN, not
a top-level section across multiple templates. PRINCIPLES is not
touched (no new principle introduced). WORKFLOW is not touched (no
new state-machine or pipeline change). Crate-level DESIGN templates
are not touched (the bench bin is not an arvo crate; it is a
sibling under `mock/benches/`).
