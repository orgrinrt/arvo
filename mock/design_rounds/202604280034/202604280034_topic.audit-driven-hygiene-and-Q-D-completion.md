### Q-D Round 7 completion: kill the std-parallel survivors

Round 202604271346's pass 7 deleted `Uint5/Uint6/Uint7/Int7/Int13` but kept `Uint8/Uint16/Uint32/Uint64` + `Int8/Int16/Int32/Int64` as "std-parallel canonical aliases". The decision: delete these too. The user's framing: retaining std-parallel sugar undermines the entire point of the substrate, which is to think in exact bits, not in std-primitive buckets. Consumer call sites use `Uint<N, S>` / `Int<N, S>` generic forms exclusively.

Concrete deletion list: `Uint8`, `Uint16`, `Uint32`, `Uint64`, `Int8`, `Int16`, `Int32`, `Int64` from `arvo/src/aliases.rs`. arvo facade `lib.rs` re-export list pruned. Audit consumers across all 16 arvo crates plus any test files; rewrite to `Uint<N, S>` / `Int<N, S>`.

### arvo-narrow-contracts merge into arvo-bits-contracts

Per the perf-expert audit and the Q-B audit rule from the just-closed round (collapse contract crates that 100% co-occur in consumers): `arvo-narrow-contracts` ships a single trait declaration (`Narrow<T>` plus the `Narrowed<N, T>` alias). Consumers of `Narrow` always also use `BitLogic` (which lives in arvo-bits-contracts) for the default-body composition. The merge collapses one crate from the workspace without changing transitive footprint.

Concrete steps:
- Move trait declaration (`Narrow<T>`, `Narrowed<N, T>` alias) from `arvo-narrow-contracts/src/lib.rs` into `arvo-bits-contracts/src/lib.rs`.
- Delete the `arvo-narrow-contracts` crate directory and workspace member entry.
- Update every consumer: `use arvo_narrow_contracts::Narrow` → `use arvo_bits_contracts::Narrow`. Concrete consumer crates: `arvo-narrow` (still empty post-Round-A), the arvo facade, possibly arvo-bitmask once Narrow impls land. Verify in-round.
- Update `mockspace.toml` `[primitive-introductions]` registration, `[lints.forbidden-imports]` scopes, and any agent rule under `.claude/rules/lint-forbidden-*.md` (regenerated by `cargo mock` after the toml change).
- Crate count goes from 16 to 15.
